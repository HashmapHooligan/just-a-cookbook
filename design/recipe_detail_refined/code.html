Perfect Weekend Sourdough

A high-hydration, naturally leavened rustic loaf with a deeply caramelized crust and open, airy crumb. Requires a 24-hour cycle.

list_alt Instructions

1

Build the Levain

In a tall jar, mix the mature starter, bread flour, whole wheat flour, and warm water. Ensure no dry spots remain. Cover loosely and rest in a warm spot (75°F - 80°F) for 5-6 hours, or until it has doubled in volume and passes the float test.

hourglass_top 5 Hours 30 Mins play_arrow
2

Autolyse

One hour before the levain is ready, mix the bread flour and whole wheat flour for the main dough in a large bowl. Add 350g of the warm water (reserve 25g for mixing the salt later). Mix until a shaggy dough forms. Cover and let rest for 1 hour.

3

Mix the Dough

Add 100g of the active levain to the autolysed dough. Dimple it in with wet fingers. Sprinkle the fine sea salt over the top and add the reserved 25g of water. Squeeze and fold the dough to integrate everything completely. Transfer to a clean tub for bulk fermentation.

4

Bulk Fermentation & Folds

Maintain dough temperature around 78°F. Perform 4 sets of stretch and folds spaced 30 minutes apart. After the last fold, let the dough rest untouched for the remainder of bulk fermentation (about 2 more hours) until it is bubbly, jiggly, and increased in volume by about 40%.

Pro Tip: Wet your hands slightly before performing stretch and folds to prevent sticking.

5

Shape and Retard

Turn dough out onto an unfloured surface. Preshape lightly into a round. Rest 20 mins. Final shape into a boule or batard and place seam-side up in a rice-floured banneton. Cover and place in the refrigerator for 12-16 hours for a cold retard.
